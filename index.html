Clipping Strategy
Social buzz tactic
Clipping for Social Media
Repurpose long-form content into short-form buzz across platforms
Clipping is the practice of taking the best 15–60 second moments from longer-form content — live sets, studio sessions, interviews, YouTube videos — and distributing them across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Done consistently, it creates a constant drumbeat of content without requiring constant new creation. It also surfaces Malachiii's catalog and personality to new audiences who might not seek out a full video.
Source Material
Live set recordings, studio session footage, interviews, collab BTS, Malhaus rehearsal, press moments, past YouTube content.
What to Clip
Peak crowd moments, first vocal takes, producer reactions, hard drops, funny or candid exchanges, emotional or honest monologue snippets.
Where It Goes
TikTok (primary — highest discovery), Instagram Reels (secondary — existing fan reach), YouTube Shorts (tertiary — indexed for search).
Cadence Goal
3–5 clips per week. Clips can be scheduled in advance using a week's worth of source material. Keeps the algorithm fed between release cycles.
Collab Clips
Any session with Hugel, Gordo, Keinemusik, or Rampa is prime clip material. A 10-minute session yields 5–10 clips. Cross-tag the collab artist for shared reach.
Malhaus Clips
Every Malhaus rehearsal and live performance should be filmed with clipping in mind. Stage moments, lighting tests, sound checks — all clippable. Build the mythology before the show exists.
